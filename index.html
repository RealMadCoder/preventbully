Prevent bullying.

Bullying affects everyone - those who are bullied, those who bully, and those who witness bullying.

Bullying is

bad. hurtful. disrespectful. painful. unkind.

I am discussing the topic of bullying because it is important to be aware of the negative consequences it has on people.

What do we mean bullying?

Bullying is any repeated behavior that intentionally hurts someone else. There are many forms of bullying such as
  • 1.  Physical Bullying  -  Physical harm or threats of harm to another person.
  • 2.  Verbal Bullying  -  Words used to bully others - teasing, name-calling and insults.
  • 3.  Cyberbullying  -  Bullying through the internet.
  • 4.  Social Bullying  -  Ruining/harming someone's reputation or relationships.

Bullying can have many negative effects on the victim, such as

  • Anxiety and Depression
  • Low Self-Esteem
  • Isolation
  • The victim can develop sleeping problems and eating disorders
  • In severe cases, it can lead to self-harm

However, bullies can also receive effects on themselves:

  • Difficulty forming relationships with peers
  • Isolation
  • Academic downgrades
  • Guilt and regret

So... how can we prevent bullying?

  • Tell a trusted adult  -  this could be a parent, teacher or guardian.
  • Don't be a bystander  -  if you see someone being bullied, go to them and support them.
  • Deal with the bully  -  strongly ask them to stop, report it or walk away. Don't cause a scene by retaliating.
  • Reach out  -  Contact NSPCC if you prefer to talk to someone annonymously.
This campaign reflects how God wants us to treat everyone else as we want to be treated ourselves. No one wants to be bullied.
